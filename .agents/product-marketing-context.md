## Product Overview

**One-liner:** A new way to release electronic and OPM music — without the label game, with a real human manager.

**What it does:** AIR Music Distribution delivers independent artists' music to 100+ platforms (Spotify, Apple Music, YouTube Music, TikTok, Beatport) with a personal manager assigned to each project. Unlike DIY platforms (DistroKid, TuneCore), every artist gets a dedicated human contact, YouTube Content ID monetization, and revenue-share economics with no upfront fees.

**Product category:** Music distribution + artist services (managed/curated tier — between mass DIY and label-grade)

**Product type:** Service (managed distribution with revenue share)

**Business model:**
- Revenue split 70/30 (artist/AIR)
- $0 setup fee, no subscriptions, no recoupment
- First payout in 3-4 months (DSP delay), monthly thereafter
- Curated/selective intake (not mass distribution)

## Problems & Pain Points

**Core problem:** Independent electronic/OPM artists are stuck between two bad options — mass DIY platforms that treat them like inventory (DistroKid, TuneCore) and traditional labels that ignore them or trap them with bad terms. Neither provides real human partnership, Content ID monetization, or rights retention.

**Why alternatives fall short:**
- **DistroKid/TuneCore:** Automated support, no human, no Content ID, no curation = artist feels invisible
- **Traditional labels:** 2% acceptance rate, lose rights, slow communication, advance recoupment traps
- **CD Baby:** Declining since acquisition, still expensive (9% cut)
- **Symphonic:** Closed doors, invite-only

**What it costs them:**
- Lost YouTube Content ID revenue (DJs use tracks in mixes — artist sees $0)
- Spotify 1K-stream threshold (2024+) means tracks under 1,000 streams earn nothing
- Months of waiting on label responses that never come
- Royalty confusion — can't tell what they're owed

**Emotional tension:**
- "Not because my track isn't good enough — because no one listened"
- Frustration of being rejected by labels but having nowhere clearly better to go
- Anxiety about signing rights away vs. staying invisible
- Feeling like a number in a system

## Competitive Landscape

**Direct (same solution, same problem):**
- **AWAL** (invite-only, premium) — falls short because closed access, hard to get in
- **Identity Music, Symphonic** — falls short because corporate/closed
- **Underdog Music** — niche, mostly UK/US focus

**Secondary (different solution, same problem):**
- **DistroKid, TuneCore, CD Baby** — falls short because no human support, no Content ID, no curation
- **Ditto Music** — slow support (24-48h), generic
- **Amuse** — automated, no real partnership

**Indirect (conflicting approach):**
- **Traditional labels** — falls short because 2% accept rate, rights ownership, slow comms
- **Doing nothing / SoundCloud only** — falls short because no monetization, no growth

**AIR's optimal position:** Strong support × Mid-range price = the only place serving mid-tier artists with real humans without going corporate.

## Differentiation

**Key differentiators:**
- Personal manager (not a queue, not a chatbot — a human who knows your catalog)
- YouTube Content ID — most distributors ignore this; AIR specializes
- Selective intake (not mass) — quality of roster matters
- Revenue share, not flat fee — aligned incentives
- 100% rights retention — never owned, ever
- 10+ years track record (AIR Media-Tech ecosystem)
- Verified partnerships: YouTube, Google, TikTok, Microsoft

**How we do it differently:** Curated human-first managed distribution at a mid-tier price point. We say no to artists we can't help — that's the value proposition.

**Why that's better:** When you're mid-tier and trying to grow, generic DIY tools can't help you scale. Labels don't want you yet. AIR fills that gap with real partnership and YouTube monetization most others ignore.

**Why customers choose us:**
- They've been rejected by labels and want a fair partnership instead
- They're losing money on YouTube and need Content ID
- They want a real person — not a ticket queue
- They want to keep their rights

## Objections

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "70/30 — that's a high cut for distribution" | We're not just distribution. You get a personal manager, Content ID monetization on YouTube (which DIY platforms don't do), and we only earn when you earn — no upfront fees, no recoupment. Compare to a label deal where you might get 15-20%. |
| "I already use DistroKid for $25/year" | If you have <500 monthly listeners, DistroKid is fine. Once you're in the mid-tier range and DJs are using your tracks in mixes, you're losing more than $25/year in unclaimed YouTube revenue. |
| "Will I lose my rights?" | No. 100% rights retention, always. We distribute, we don't own. Ever. |
| "How do I know this isn't a scam like other 'managed' services?" | 5,500+ artists in network, 10+ years on the market, verified partnerships with YouTube/Google/TikTok/Microsoft. Artists like Miss Monique (1.3M YouTube subs) have been with us 3+ years. |
| "When do I get paid?" | First payout 3-4 months (this is DSP standard reporting delay, not us). After that — monthly with full breakdown. |

**Anti-persona (NOT a good fit):**
- Beginners with 0 releases or under 500 monthly listeners — we're not a starter platform
- Artists submitting mass AI-generated content
- Material with unresolved rights, uncleared samples, or disputed beats
- Anyone trying to manipulate streaming/analytics
- Artists looking for "guaranteed" streams or playlist placements (no one can honestly guarantee that)

## Switching Dynamics (JTBD Four Forces)

**Push (away from current solution):**
- "My current distributor doesn't respond"
- "I don't see YouTube Content ID money"
- "I'm tired of waiting for label responses"
- Royalty confusion and unclear payouts
- Spotify 1K threshold making small releases earn nothing

**Pull (toward AIR):**
- A real human who responds in 24h
- YouTube Content ID — actually getting paid for DJ mixes
- 100% rights retention — no scary contracts
- 70% revenue share with no upfront cost
- Curated (not mass) — feels selective, validating

**Habit (keeps them stuck):**
- Already paid annual fee at DistroKid/TuneCore
- "It mostly works" with current platform
- Inertia of having music live somewhere
- Fear of switching distributors and losing data/playlist placements

**Anxiety (worries about switching):**
- "What if my Spotify monthly listeners drop during transfer?"
- "What if I lose my playlist placements?"
- "Is this legit or another scam?"
- "Will my existing label deal conflict?"
- 70/30 split sounds high vs flat-fee mental model

## Goals

**Business goal (Year 1, SEA):** 130 clients across PH/MY/ID
**Sub-goal (Philippines):** 3-4 new clients per month from paid acquisition

**Conversion action:**
- Primary: Submit Spotify URL + complete Typebot qualification → Qualified Lead in HubSpot
- Secondary: Test release agreement (3-5 tracks, no long-term contract)
- Final: Signed client + active release schedule

**Current funnel metrics (as of May 2026, 6 weeks of data):**
- 113 total contacts from paid ads
- Facebook: ~3% Lead → Opportunity (low; algorithm attracting beginners)
- Google: ~12% Lead → Opportunity (working — high-intent search traffic)
- Meta budget: $20/day total ($600/month)
- Google budget: ~$10/day per campaign

**Target funnel metrics (4-week optimization horizon):**
- CPQL (cost per qualified lead): <$20
- Typebot disqualification rate: >40% (filtering beginners is success)
- Lead → Opportunity rate: >15%

**Open issues blocking goal:**
1. Meta optimizing for wrong conversion event (likely click/form-start, not Typebot completion)
2. Ad copy doesn't filter beginners
3. Personal Manager Typebot has 8 questions (research says 3-4 optimal)
4. No lookalike audience based on actual AIR clients
5. No offline conversion import back to Meta/Google
