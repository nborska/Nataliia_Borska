Introduction

Why You're Reading This

You applied to AIR Music, and we had to pause your application — not because your music isn't interesting, but because of how it was made.

Fully AI-generated tracks without the right licensing create real problems on streaming platforms. Not just for us — for you. This guide exists so you understand exactly what's happening, and what your actual options are.

What Streaming Platforms Actually Check

Spotify, Apple Music, YouTube Music and most major platforms now have two layers of detection:

Layer 1 — Content ID Scanning

Every track gets scanned against a database of known AI-generated audio patterns. Suno, Udio, and similar tools have recognizable sonic fingerprints. When a match is found — the track gets flagged automatically.


Layer 2 — Rights Verification

When you upload through a distributor, you sign a declaration that you own the rights to this music. With a free Suno account, you don't. Suno's free tier gives you personal use only — not commercial distribution rights.

⚠ What happens when platforms find out

Track gets taken down (sometimes within days) · Royalties already earned get withheld · Distributor account gets suspended · In some cases — permanent ban from the platform.

This isn't a risk worth taking. And it's completely avoidable — once you know the rules.

Step-by-Step

From AI Idea to Distributable Track

1

Generate Your Reference

Use Suno, Udio, or any AI tool to create a rough version of the sound you're going for. This is your sketch, not your final track. Think of it the way a painter uses a pencil outline — it guides, it doesn't define.

2

Extract What's Yours

Write down the melody. Transcribe the chord progression. Note the structure. These ideas can be yours — AI just helped you hear them faster. The moment you write them down, they become yours.

3

Record Yourself

Even a basic voice memo of you singing the melody counts as human performance. You don't need a professional studio. You need your voice, your instrument, your performance on the final track.

4

Produce the Final Version

Rebuild the track with your performance as the foundation. AI tools for mixing, mastering, and production are completely fine at this stage — the human authorship is already established.

5

Document Everything

Keep your original prompts, sketches, and voice memos. If a platform ever questions your authorship, your creative process is your proof. A folder on your phone is enough.

Before You Submit to Any Distributor

  • You wrote the melody or lyrics (even if AI helped you develop them)
  • A human performed on the track — vocals, instrument, or both
  • You have commercial rights to any AI-generated elements used
  • You can describe your creative process if asked
  • The track doesn't contain samples you don't own

If you can check all five — you're ready to distribute. And you're ready to reapply to AIR.
