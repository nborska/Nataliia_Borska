# NOTES FOR PRESENTER

## Context: Primavera Sound 2026
- Audience: mid-tier indie artists, globally minded, already distributed
- Energy of the room: curious, sophisticated, slightly cynical (they've heard promises before)
- Goal: start conversations, not close deals from stage
- Best outcome: artists scan QR, book Free Track Safety Check, or stop by the booth

## Key messages to emphasize live:
1. **Pain first** — open with "have you been in this situation" — get heads nodding before showing the product
2. **Human first** — stress the personal manager more than the platform features
3. **No hype** — don't promise streams growth or fame; promise structure and support
4. **Qualifying** — be explicit that this is not for beginners; it makes the offer MORE attractive to mid-tier artists

## Lines that land (tested messaging):
- *"You're not a beginner. But you don't feel like a professional either."*
- *"We earn when you earn."*
- *"This is not about talent. It's about structure."*
- *"When something goes wrong — you have someone to call."*

## What NOT to say:
- ❌ "Upload your music everywhere"
- ❌ "Grow your streams fast"
- ❌ "All-in-one platform"
- ❌ "Best distribution on the market"
- ❌ Any direct competitor name-drops

## Objection handling:
**"I already have a distributor"** → "Perfect. That means you know what's missing. Let's do a free audit — 10 minutes and you'll know exactly what you're losing."

**"Why should I trust you?"** → "14 years. Official YouTube partner. And every artist gets a manager with a real name, not a ticket number. Come talk to the team."

**"What's the catch?"** → "Revenue share. We make money when you make money. There's no upfront. That's the whole model."
